Preliminary Published Research

Using a subset of the preliminary research we conducted for our Harry Frank Guggenheim grant application, we published an accessible version of our findings in the Washington Post and an abridged version of our methodology on GitHub.

Like we discuss in our application, the Harry Frank Guggenheim Foundation grant would allow us to expand this research and make it even more accessible and useful for other researchers, academics, and the general public.


Dissemination of Research Findings

We hope that the Harry Frank Guggenheim foundation will consider the DroneScout website, and the server expenses associated with it, to not be in violation of their policy to not support the dissemination of research findings with grant funds.

While we understand and agree with the motiviations and reasoning to enforce such a policy against publication and conference fees, we believe the DroneScout website is an exception to this rule, as it is evidently different from using research funds for conference travel or publication fees.

We consider the DroneScout website, while untraditional, an essential aspect of our research: it will support our strike search, government analysis, and strike multimedia collection.
